# Concept Cracking (פיצוח קונספט)

The concept is decided **before** design and build. It is derived from the source — the content dictates the metaphor. This file is the method for finding it.

## Step 1 — Read for the "shape" of the content
Before naming anything, read the whole source and ask:
- What is the learner supposed to *do differently* after this? (the real objective, not the stated one)
- What is the emotional core? (fear of getting it wrong? pride in doing it right? curiosity?)
- Is the content procedural (steps), conceptual (ideas), regulatory (rules), or situational (judgment calls)?
- Who is the learner, and what is their day actually like?

## Step 2 — Find the metaphor (מטאפורה ויזואלית)
A strong metaphor makes abstract content tangible and gives every screen a reason to look the way it does. Let the content suggest it:

| If the content is about… | Metaphor that often fits |
|---|---|
| Investigation, privacy, finding violations | תיק חקירה (investigation file / case folder) |
| Procedures, safety steps, doing it right | מערכת בקרה / control room, checklist board |
| Risk, hazards, consequences | game with stakes, "spot the hazard" environment |
| Process over time, onboarding | מסע / journey, map with stops |
| Choices and judgment | branching scenario, "a day in the life" |

Rules for the metaphor:
- It must hold across **all** modules, not just the intro.
- It must not fight the seriousness of the content (a playful game metaphor on a harassment-prevention lomda needs careful handling — dignity first).
- It drives the visual system: palette, typography, icons, screen furniture, transitions.

## Step 3 — Set the tone & voice (טון ושפה)
- Active, accessible, human. Speaks *to* the learner, not *at* them.
- Decide: formal vs. warm, second person vs. neutral, how much humor (usually little, and never on sensitive topics).
- The tone is consistent with the metaphor (a "case file" speaks like a calm investigator, not a cheerleader).

## Step 4 — Define the narrative arc (מבנה נרטיבי)
- A beginning that sets stakes (why this matters to *you*), tension (a problem, a risk, a dilemma), and resolution (mastery / the right way).
- The arc carries the learner module-to-module so the lomda feels like one thing, not a stack of slides.

## Step 5 — Name it and check alignment
Write a 3-4 line concept statement:
> **Concept:** [metaphor name]. The learner is [role within the metaphor]. The lomda speaks [tone]. The arc goes from [start] → [tension] → [resolution].

Then verify: do visual, tone, and narrative all point the same way? If any one fights the others, fix it now. **Present this to the user for approval before designing or building anything.**

## Anti-patterns
- A decorative metaphor that disappears after the intro screen.
- A concept imposed top-down that the source material resists.
- "Concept" reduced to a color scheme. It is metaphor + tone + narrative, together.
